Pasta

Pasta aglio e olio. Dirt cheap but very tasty.

    Ingredients:
  • 1 lb dried linguine
  • 12 cloves garlic
    • 4 minced
    • 4 thinly sliced
    • 4 smashed
  • Red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper

    • Tools:
  • 1 really big pot
  • 10 inch stainless steel pan
  • Western chef's knife
    Steps:
  1. Put about 1.5 quarts of water in the pot. Salt it until it tastes like ocean water,
    then heat it until boiling. Once at a boil, add the linguine and cook just shy of doneness
    about one minute before the package says so. You can take a bite and test for texture.
    Strain in the sink, reserving about a cup of pasta water.
  2. Pour olive oil in pan and place the 4 smashed cloves in, then heat on high. Once it
    starts to sizzle, turn the heat to low. Cook for about 5 to 7 minutes until garlic has browned.
    Discard cloves once its done.
  3. Turn heat to high on the pan. Add sliced garlic and red pepper flakes and cook until garlic
    is pale brown, around 45 seconds. Then add minced garlic and cook until fragrant, around 30 seconds.
    Remove from the heat and add butter, stirring until melted.
  4. Return linguine to the pot and heat over medium low. Pour the garlicky oil over the noodles and stir
    to combine. Add salt and pepper to taste.
